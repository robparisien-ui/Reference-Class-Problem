How fast do I learn?
λ — learning curve decay constant Published CUSUM plateaus (43–100 cases) imply λ ≈ 0.03–0.07. Higher values model exceptionally fast learners (λ = 0.30 ≈ proficiency by case 10).

How bad is a serious complication?
Wh — complication severity Anchored to reoperation probability: periprosthetic fractures require reoperation in nearly all cases vs. ~20–30% for dislocations, yielding a base ratio of ≈3. This is conservative — reoperation alone understates the additional morbidity of fracture revision.
3.0

Three parameters are held constant: benefit of a prevented dislocation = 1 (reference unit); serious complication rate at case 1 = 7% (Dall’Oca, Foissey, Shen); baseline rate once proficient = 2% (Kahn, Lygrisse).

Each curve shows cumulative incremental net utility for a surgeon transitioning from PA to DAA, stratified by annual case volume. The shaded region below zero represents net harm to the cumulative patient population relative to continuing with PA. Filled circles mark the break-even point for each volume tier. Harm term = excess serious complication rate above PA baseline × Wh, decaying exponentially to zero at proficiency. Dislocation is handled by the NNT benefit term; LFCN and minor wound events are excluded from the harm term.

Parameter key

NNTHow many cases of DAA needed to prevent one additional dislocation over modern PA with capsule repair. Higher = smaller benefit per case.
λHow quickly the serious complication rate decays toward baseline. Proficiency is reached at approximately 3/λ cases: λ = 0.03 → ~100 cases; λ = 0.05 → ~60 cases; λ = 0.07 → ~43 cases; λ = 0.15 → ~20 cases; λ = 0.30 → ~10 cases.
WhHow many prevented dislocations a single serious complication “costs.” Default of 3 is anchored to the ratio of reoperation probabilities: periprosthetic fractures require reoperation in nearly all cases vs. ~20–30% for dislocations. This is conservative; reoperation alone understates the full morbidity difference.
FixedBenefit reference unit = 1. Initial serious complication rate = 7%. Baseline (proficient) rate = 2%. See parameter sources below.

How to explore the model

NNTSlide left toward 20 to model older literature (inflated benefit). Slide right toward 100–200 for modern high-quality evidence. Watch how the break-even horizon shifts.
WhDefault of 3 is a conservative lower bound based on reoperation ratios. Increase toward 4–5 to account for the full morbidity gap between fracture revision and closed reduction. This is the most sensitive parameter.
λSlide left for a slower learner (~100 cases to proficiency in a community setting without mentorship). Slide right for a faster one (~10 cases for an exceptionally fast learner). Even at the fastest plausible learning rate, low-volume surgeons face extended break-even horizons.
VolumesSet V1–V4 to reflect your institution’s volume tiers. The key question: at your annual volume, does the curve ever cross zero within a realistic career horizon?
Curves above the zero line = net benefit to your cumulative patient population. Shaded region below zero = net harm.
